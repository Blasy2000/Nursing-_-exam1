Nursing Exam: Vital Signs and Injections

True/False Questions

Question 1:

Blood pressure is recorded in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).

Answer: True

Rationale: Blood pressure is commonly measured in mmHg as it represents the pressure exerted by blood on the walls of arteries.

Question 2:

The radial artery is the preferred site for assessing heart rate in adults.

Answer: True

Rationale: The radial artery, located on the wrist, is often the most accessible and easiest site to check a patientâ€™s heart rate.

Question 3:

Intramuscular (IM) injections are administered at a 90-degree angle.

Answer: True

Rationale: IM injections are given at a 90-degree angle to ensure the medication is delivered deep into the muscle tissue.

Question 4:

Subcutaneous (SC) injections should be administered into muscle tissue.

Answer: False

Rationale: Subcutaneous injections are intended for the fatty tissue beneath the skin, not for muscles.

Multiple Choice Questions

Question 5:

What is the normal range for adult systolic blood pressure?

  • A) 80-120 mmHg
  • B) 90-140 mmHg
  • C) 100-160 mmHg
  • D) 70-130 mmHg

Answer: B) 90-140 mmHg

Rationale: The normal range for systolic blood pressure in adults is between 90 and 140 mmHg. Any value below or above may indicate hypotension or hypertension, respectively.

Question 6:

Which of the following is the correct site for an intradermal (ID) injection?

  • A) Deltoid muscle
  • B) Subcutaneous tissue of the abdomen
  • C) Inner forearm
  • D) Vastus lateralis muscle

Answer: C) Inner forearm

Rationale: The inner forearm is the preferred site for intradermal injections, often used for allergy and tuberculosis tests.

Question 7:

Which injection type is most appropriate for administering insulin?

  • A) Intravenous (IV)
  • B) Intramuscular (IM)
  • C) Subcutaneous (SC)
  • D) Intradermal (ID)

Answer: C) Subcutaneous (SC)

Rationale: Insulin is administered subcutaneously to ensure slow and consistent absorption into the bloodstream.

Question 8:

Which of the following is an appropriate needle size for an intramuscular injection?

  • A) 18-20 gauge
  • B) 21-23 gauge
  • C) 25-27 gauge
  • D) 30-31 gauge

Answer: B) 21-23 gauge

Rationale: For intramuscular injections, a larger needle size (21-23 gauge) is used to penetrate the muscle tissue.

Question 9:

Which angle is appropriate for administering a subcutaneous injection?

  • A) 90 degrees
  • B) 45 degrees
  • C) 15 degrees
  • D) 180 degrees

Answer: B) 45 degrees

Rationale: Subcutaneous injections are typically administered at a 45-degree angle to ensure the medication is delivered to the fatty tissue beneath the skin.
